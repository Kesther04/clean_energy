Climate Change and Clean Energy

Climate Change

This is the long-term shifts in avaerage weather patterns, such as temperature and rainfall, over decades or more. It’s the longer-term trend that distinguishes it from day-to-day weather variations. Essentially, it’s about the Earth’s climate changing in a significant way over a sustained period. Climate change can be caused by volcanic eruptions, solar radiation and sunspot activity, earthly orbital changes, ocean current, greenhouse gases and deforestation.

Deforestation

Deforestation contributes up to 20 - 25% of global greenhouse
gas emission and reduces the planet's ability to absorb CO₂.

Greenhouse gas emission

The burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas is the
largest source of man-made gas emissions.

Ocean current

Ocean currents are massive flows of seawater that move heat
around the planet, regulating the Earth's climate.

Earthly orbital changes

Earth's orbits change from circular to elliptical every 100,000
years and wobble like a spinning top over 26,000 years.

Solar radiation

The sun follows approximately 11 years of sunspot activity,
alternating between periods of high and low solar radiation.

Volcano eruption

The 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo in the Philippines lowered
global temperatures by about 0.5°C (0.9°F) for nearly two years.

Clean Energy The Solution?

Of course! Affordable and clean energy sources that are renewable, environmentally friendly, and accessible to everyone can tackle the issue. These include solar, wind, hydro, and bioenergy sources, solutions that are vital in slowing down climate change and reducing pollution.

Why does it matter?

Clean energy reduces harmful emissions, supports sustainable development, and is becoming more cost-effective worldwide. These solutions can protect our planet while improving lives, conserving environmental resources, and creating jobs.

Improves Public Health

Clean energy reduces harmful emissions caused by smog and toxins that lead to air pollution, thereby improving the health of individuals.

Protect Natural Resources

Clean energy uses renewable resources like sunlight and wind, and not coal or oil, thereby conserving natural resources.

Creates Green Jobs

Solar, wind, and bioenergy industries generate employment opportunities globally ranging from agriculture to water and energy management.

Reduces Carbon Emissions

Switching to clean energy tends to cut down greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels by providing low-carbon alternatives like solar and wind.
